About

I am a Postdoc working in Daniella Rempe's group in the Jackson School of Geosciences at UT Austin. I completed my PhD with Bill Dietrich in the Earth and Planetary Science Department at UC Berkeley, where I was also advised by Todd Dawson in the Department of Integrative Biology. I previously completed a MSc in Geology and Geophysics with Cliff Riebe at the University of Wyoming.

In January, 2020, I will become an assistant professor in the Department of Geography at Simon Fraser University in Burnaby, British Columbia. Please get in touch about research opportunities there!

Research

The combined action of uplift and erosion brings bedrock toward Earth’s surface. As it approaches, weathering liberates nutrients and generates porosity, creating voids where infiltrating precipitation becomes stored and accessible to plants. My work seeks to understand the consequences of weathering—which can extend deep into the bedrock that underlies hillslopes—for water storage, runoff, erosion and ecosystem processes, and how and why weathering profiles vary across landscapes. I look across gradients of rock type, climate, and biomes, thereby turning landscapes into natural laboratories for scientific study. This approach results in transdisciplinary studies that integrate concepts from the fields of hydrology, geomorphology, and plant ecophysiology.
